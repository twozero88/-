BTC in a minute

Bitcoin is a blockchain ... based decentralized crypto-currency controlled by the people. It was brought to existince by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008 and has been running smoothly ever since.

  • Bitcoins have a limited supply of around 21M.
  • Bitcoin miners verify the transaction and mint some new coins in the process.
  • Everyone can store and see list of all bitcoin transaction that have happened in the past, but those transactions can't (very easily) be linked with person's real life identity.
  • You need no id/verification to send and receive btc.
  • Anyone can make simple transactions to send and receive money withouting knowing the actual working.
  • BTC rates are subject to markets. One may buy/sell any time and store the coins in a wallet (hardware or software)
  • One can use bitcoin transactions ranging from a few cents to millions of dollars without depending on any third party. Distance between senders and receivers is immaterial.
  • This is also the reason why many countries may have their own laws regarding cryptocurrencies.
